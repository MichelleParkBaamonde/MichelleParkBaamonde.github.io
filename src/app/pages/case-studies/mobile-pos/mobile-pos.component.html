Optimizing usability of the mobile application for an automotive retailer

Timeline: May 2023 - March 2024

Role: Design Lead

Team: Principal UX Designer (1), Design Lead (2), Associate UX Designer (2), UX Product Analyst (1)

Problem
To speed up delivery of the Mobile POS application, usability testing was removed from the original timeline, despite the UX team’s recommendation to conduct testing. The application was missing real employee feedback prior to launch.
Solution
Gather actionable user insights to improve the overall user experience of the Mobile POS application and align it with the needs and expectations of the client's store employees.
Approach
Prioritized developing prototypes and conducting usability testing while still meeting original design deadlines.
1. Prototype Development
Phase 1 (May-Sep 2023)
Built the first prototype of a real in-store scenario to give stakeholders the opportunity to interact with a visual representation of screens from different applications working together.
Phase 2 (Oct 2023-Mar 2024)
Designed multiple paths within three new prototypes to account for additional scenarios and various parts of a screen a user might interact with.
2. Research Plan
Helped create detailed plans containing objectives, recruiting strategy, timeline, questionnaires, and scripts for two phases of research.
3. Stakeholder Management
Communicated to stakeholders the importance of observing store employees to gain real feedback from the users that will be utilizing the application.
4. Advocate for the User
Strongly advocated for moderated testing with a mobile device to set up a more realistic testing environment for participating store employees.

Phase 2

Based on the success of Phase 1, we expanded the original prototype to conduct a second round of tests.

  • Brainstormed, iterated, and built 3 new prototypes that incorporated parts of the app that were not tested in Phase 1 to get new feedback.

  • Pushed for moderated in-person testing with a mobile device to test the app in a more realistic environment with the ability to pick up on body language and ask follow-up questions in real-time.

  • Ran practice sessions with corporate team members to ensure prototypes reflect realistic in-store scenarios and incorporated their feedback to finalize the prototypes.

  • Conducted 10 moderated usability tests with store employees using a Zebra TC77 mobile device.

Impact
  • Results will be used to prioritize features for development and to inform testing in the soft launch for the first ten stores before it is released to all 1,100+ stores across the US.
  • Increases revenue due to improved store efficiency and productivity.
  • Sets a blueprint for future usability testing by allowing the business to see the value of research in the design and delivery process.
