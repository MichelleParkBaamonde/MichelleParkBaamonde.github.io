Redesigning the Customer Service List application for an automotive retailer

Timeline: Sep 2023 - January 2024

Role: Design Lead

Team: Design Lead (1), Associate UX Designer (2), UX Product Analyst (1)

Problem
After the initial launch of the Desktop POS, the business wanted to make significant modifications to the Customer Service List design, including a request to vary dashboard views based on store type.
Solution
Reimagine how the main POS application looks and functions to enhance a store employee’s experience with the POS system and make it easier to provide services to customers.
Approach
Combed through a large list of change requests, ideated on ways to incorporate business requirements and sponsor user feedback. Whiteboarded, iterated, and conducted multiple presentations to deliver final designs by a strict deadline.
Requirement #1
There needs to be a bolder differentiation between service types (Appointment, Walk-in, BOPIS, Return for Service).
Requirement #2
Add new information to the customer cards and allow employees to see more cards on the screen at once.
Design
Incorporated color-coded flags at the top of a customer card to make it easier for employees to spot them at a glance.
Design
Introduced collapsed and expanded views and rearranged items based on priority for both views.
Feedback/Changes
Iterated on color combinations to differentiate services while staying close to color coding the employees are familiar with.
Feedback/Changes
Created [text/color/icons] variants of how and where to present info. Consulted stakeholders on what is most important for employees to see at different phases of their workflow.
Old Version
The customer cards contained a lot of extra whitespace and their large size meant employees could only view a few at a time. For busy stores with a long list of customers, it would be difficult for employees to view details at a glance.
New Version
Added color-coded indicators, re-arranged details based on priority, and implemented a more effective use of space. Improves the store’s workflow by allowing employees to see more customers at once and focus on specific customer details via expand/collapse.
Impact
  • The new Customer Service List for Desktop was rolled out to 6 store locations and will roll out to all 1,100+ stores across the US in 2025.
  • Increases revenue due to improved store efficiency and productivity.
